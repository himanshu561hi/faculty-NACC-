NAAC ACCREDITATION DOCUMENTATION MANUAL - CRITERIA OVERVIEW

CRITERION 2 - TEACHING-LEARNING AND EVALUATION
Criterion 2 focuses on how the institution ensures student diversity, caters to learning levels, implements ICT-based student-centric systems, maintains teacher profile quality, reform examinations, and measures learning outcome attainment.
Key Metric 2.6.1: The institution states and displays Programme Outcomes (POs) and Course Outcomes (COs) for all programs on its public website. Teachers and students are fully aware of these outcomes. The IQAC conducts annual awareness workshops for first-year students and regular audits of website disclosures.
Attainment is evaluated based on direct assessments (internal midterms, semester-end examinations, laboratory records) and indirect assessments (student exit surveys, employer feedback, placement ratios).

CRITERION 3 - RESEARCH, INNOVATIONS AND EXTENSION
Criterion 3 reviews the promotion of research, resource mobilization, incubation ecosystem, patent filings, research papers indexed in UGC-CARE list, and community extension outreach projects.
Metric 3.6.1: Extension activities are carried out in the neighborhood community, sensitizing students to social issues, for their holistic development, and impact thereof. The institution partners with active NGOs and local Panchayats to run sanitation, literacy, health, and clean energy campaigns. 
Supporting evidence requires: detailed annual reports, student attendance sheets signed by the program officer, photographs with dates and captions, and awards received from government bodies.

CRITERION 5 - STUDENT SUPPORT AND PROGRESSION
Criterion 5 reviews the availability of student welfare schemes, scholarships, placement assistance, higher education progression, and registered alumni association contributions.
Metric 5.4.1: The institution must show an active, registered Alumni Association that contributes significantly through financial and non-financial support services. Financial contributions must be backed by audited statements, whereas non-financial support includes guest lectures, mentoring, and reference library donations.
Required evidence checklist for Criterion 5:
- Scholarship Records: copies of government and non-government scholarship certificates, student lists, and ledger records.
- Placement Reports: corporate recruitment letters, salary slips of placed students, and student lists showing employer name.
- Capacity Building Schemes: brochures, reports, and dated photographs of soft-skills, life-skills, and computing workshops.
- Alumni Engagement Records: registration certificates, audited alumni association financial records, and lists of mentoring sessions.
