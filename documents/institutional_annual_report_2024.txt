STATE UNIVERSITY OF TECHNOLOGY AND SCIENCE - ANNUAL REPORT 2024

1. GENERAL PROFILE & RATIOS
- Total student enrollment in academic year 2023-2024 stands at 4,820 students across all undergraduate and postgraduate programs.
- The university has 241 full-time faculty members, maintaining a highly healthy Student-to-Teacher Ratio of 20:1.
- Total full-time faculty members holding a Ph.D. is 185 (76.7% of the total faculty strength).

2. PLACEMENT & STUDENT PROGRESSION (CRITERION 5)
- During the 2023-24 placement drive, the university achieved a record-breaking Placement Percentage of 88.5%, with 682 outgoing students placed in leading national and multinational corporations.
- The highest salary package achieved was INR 24.5 LPA, and the average salary package stood at INR 6.8 LPA.
- Progression to higher education: 94 outgoing students successfully progressed to prestigious national and international research universities for M.S. and Ph.D. programs.

3. RESEARCH, INNOVATION & EXTENSION (CRITERION 3)
- Research Publications: In calendar year 2024, the faculty published a total of 142 research publications in UGC-CARE, Scopus, and Web of Science indexed journals.
- Seed Money: The university disbursed a total of INR 24.5 Lakhs in seed money grants to 12 junior faculty members in 2024 to support their preliminary research and pilot studies.
- Extramural Grants: Received INR 1.2 Crores in research grants from government bodies (DST, DBT, and ISRO) for ongoing environmental sensing projects.
- Innovation Ecosystem: The university incubation center 'SUTS-Spark Hub' registered 6 student-led start-ups and successfully filed 8 new patents in 2024.

4. INFRASTRUCTURE & LEARN RESOURCES (CRITERION 4)
- Student-to-Computer Ratio: SUTS provides 964 desktop terminals in active computer laboratories, resulting in a Student-to-Computer Ratio of 5:1.
- Campus IT updates: The entire campus was updated to high-speed optical fiber Wi-Fi in 2024, raising the internet bandwidth to 1 Gbps with redundant backups.
- Infrastructure Maintenance: The audited balance sheet records an expenditure of INR 84.6 Lakhs spent on the maintenance of physical facilities and INR 52.3 Lakhs spent on academic support facilities.

5. BEST PRACTICES (CRITERION 7)
- Practice Title: Eco-Campus Decentralized Zero-Waste Initiative.
- Objective: Reduce solid waste dumps, treat greywater, and eliminate plastic.
- Details: Installed a decentralized solar-powered sewage treatment plant (STP) with a daily capacity of 50,000 liters (50 KLD). The treated water is fully reused for campus horticulture and gardening.
- Solar Infrastructure: Installed rooftop solar photovoltaic panels with a total capacity of 150 kW on the main academic block and central library. This solar setup offset 22% of the university's annual electricity grid consumption.
- Waste recycling: Recycled 1,200 kg of organic kitchen waste from hostels into high-grade compost using automated composting drums.
